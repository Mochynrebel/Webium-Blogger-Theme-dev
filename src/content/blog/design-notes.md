An editorial homepage has one job: help readers choose a good next page.

That sounds simple, but it usually fails in two ways. Some homepages bury the newest writing under decorative clutter. Others strip away all personality and become interchangeable.

The better middle ground is deliberate:

- one clear featured story
- a small set of recent articles
- strong category and tag signals
- a calm layout with enough contrast

For Webium, that meant keeping the reading-first mood while making the front page easier to scan on both desktop and mobile.
